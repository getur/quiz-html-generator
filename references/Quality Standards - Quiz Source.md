# Quality Standards for Educational Content in Mozambique

## Module: Quality Standards
## Lesson: Production Standards for Video, Audio, Interactive Content and Assessment

---

## 1. Video Characteristics

### Resolution and Bandwidth
- Videos must have a minimum resolution of 720p and be optimized for low bandwidth.
- Compressed settings should be used so the video loads on slow connections.
- Delivering only large files that won't load on weak internet must be avoided.

### Aspect Ratio
- The aspect ratio must be 16:9 with no black bars on any edge.
- Full screen 16:9 is required. Black bars on top, bottom or sides of the video are not acceptable.

### Device Testing
- Videos must be tested on mobile, tablet, and desktop with screenshots attached.
- Testing on only one device or screen size is not sufficient.

### Narration
- Professional, clean narration in standard Portuguese is required.
- The narrator must speak clearly in Mozambican Portuguese at a steady pace.
- Using Brazilian Portuguese words or a rushed, unclear delivery must be avoided.

### File Format
- File format must be MP4 or HEVC.
- Export as MP4 or HEVC. Confirm the file plays correctly before delivering.
- Delivering in AVI, MOV, WMV or other formats is not acceptable.

### Visual Relevance
- Concepts must be clearly explained using visuals relevant to Mozambique.
- Images and footage from Mozambique that match what is being explained must be used.
- Images with no connection to Mozambique or the topic being explained are not acceptable.

### Subtitles
- Mandatory Portuguese subtitles must be included.
- Subtitles must be kept in the same fixed position, timed correctly throughout.
- No subtitles, incorrect timing, or subtitles that change position are not acceptable.

### Video Length
- Videos must not exceed 10 minutes per lesson.
- Each lesson video must be kept to 10 minutes or under. Split longer content into separate videos.
- Single videos longer than 10 minutes without a break are not acceptable.

### File Size
- File size must be under 50 MB.
- Compress the video to keep the file size under 50 MB.
- Large files that take too long to download on low bandwidth are not acceptable.

### On-Screen Text Readability
- On-screen text must be readable on mobile devices.
- Large, clear fonts must be used for any text shown on screen. Test readability on a small phone screen.
- Small or low-contrast on-screen text that cannot be read on a phone is not acceptable.

### Subtitle Consistency
- Subtitles must be consistent, correctly timed, and align with narration.
- Subtitle timing must be checked against the narrator's speech at multiple points, especially after cuts. Font, size and position must be consistent throughout.
- Subtitles that jump position, change font, appear too early/late, or fall out of sync with the speaker are not acceptable.

### Visual Quality
- Resolution and visual quality must be clear throughout.
- The full video must be reviewed for quality drops, pixelation or blurry frames. Re-export any degraded sections.
- Sections with noticeably lower quality, blurry frames or encoding artefacts are not acceptable.

### Pacing
- Video pacing must be appropriate for the learner.
- Enough time must be allowed on each concept for the learner to absorb. Cramming too many visuals in a short span must be avoided.
- Rushing through content or showing too many images and videos in a short time is not acceptable.

### Background Music
- Background music must be appropriate and at a suitable volume.
- Background music must be kept low so narration is always clearly audible. Remove music that adds no learning value.
- Loud background music that competes with narration or distracts the learner is not acceptable.

### Visual Alignment
- Visuals must directly support specific learning points.
- Each visual should illustrate the specific point being narrated. What is said must align with what is shown.
- Generic or repeated visuals that do not match the narration or learning objective are not acceptable.

### Infographics
- Infographics and visual learning cues must be used effectively.
- Infographics, on-screen text and visual cues must be used to reinforce key concepts. Explain simply rather than reading from a textbook.
- Narration that reads directly from a manual with no visual support or simplification is not acceptable.

## 2. Audio

### Audio Quality
- No strange or broken sounds in the audio are permitted.
- The full audio must be listened to before sending. Fix any strange sounds.
- Background noise, distortion or sudden silences are not acceptable.

### Audio-Visual Sync
- Audio and visuals must be in sync.
- The voice must be checked against the video at several points, especially after cuts.
- Voice that is out of sync with what is shown on screen is not acceptable.

### Narrator Consistency
- Narrator tone must be consistent throughout the video.
- The same voice and sound quality must be kept from start to finish.
- Sections that sound noticeably different, as if recorded separately, are not acceptable.

## 3. Video / Content Flow

### Clear Lesson Format
- The lesson format must be clear to the learner.
- A clear structure must be followed: intro, content, conclusion. The learner always knows where they are.
- Jumping between topics with no clear order or structure is not acceptable.

### Introduction
- The lesson must introduce what the lesson is about.
- The learner must be told what the lesson is about at the start.
- Going straight into content without any introduction is not acceptable.

### Conclusion
- A conclusion must be provided at the end.
- End with a short summary. Use the same closing style across all videos.
- Ending abruptly with no summary, or different endings on each video, are not acceptable.

### Section Introductions
- Each new section must have an introduction.
- The start of each new section must be signaled with a title or verbal cue.
- Moving to a new topic with no warning or marker is not acceptable.

### Visual Safety
- Flashing images, overwhelming motion or heavy animations must be avoided.
- Simple cuts, fades or transitions must be used. Each scene must be given enough time to be read and understood. Every animation must serve a learning purpose.
- Fast cuts, flashing images, autoplay animations, or heavy motion effects that distract or disappear before the learner can read them are not acceptable.

### On-Screen Text Consistency
- On-screen text must be used consistently throughout the video.
- On-screen text (titles, labels, key terms) must be used at every relevant point in the video to reinforce learning, not just in one section.
- On-screen text that only appears briefly or in a single sub-section near the end is not acceptable. It must be used consistently throughout.

## 4. Quiz / Assessment Characteristics

### Curriculum Alignment
- Quizzes must be aligned with national curriculum learning outcomes.
- Each question should link to a specific learning outcome from the national curriculum.
- Questions with no clear connection to the curriculum are not acceptable.

### Variety of Question Types
- A variety of question types must be included.
- Multiple question types must be used: multiple-choice, true/false, short answer, matching and drag-and-drop.
- Using only one question type throughout the quiz is not acceptable.

### Assessment Levels
- Assessment levels must include: Recall, Understanding, Application.
- Questions must test memory, understanding and real-life use of knowledge.
- Only asking questions that test basic recall is not acceptable.

### Offline Functionality
- Quizzes must work online and offline.
- The quiz must be tested in offline mode. Everything must work without internet.
- Content that requires an internet connection to work is not acceptable.

### Time and Language
- No time-based penalties unless pedagogically necessary.
- Learners must be allowed to answer at their own pace. Questions must be short, simple and in Mozambican Portuguese.
- Rules that cut a learner's score for taking too long, long or confusing questions, Brazilian Portuguese, or double negatives are not acceptable.

### Answer Verdict
- The quiz must provide written and spoken verdict on correct/incorrect answers.
- A clear correct/incorrect result must be shown after each answer, both as written text and spoken aloud.
- Only showing results at the end, or without narration, or the learner being unsure if their answer was right are not acceptable.

### Answer Explanations
- Each answer must include a written and spoken explanation.
- After each question, a brief explanation of why the answer is correct or incorrect must be provided, both as on-screen text and narrated audio.
- No explanation after answering, or the learner only seeing correct/incorrect with no reasoning, are not acceptable.

### Consistency Across Questions
- Questions must not contradict each other.
- All questions must be reviewed together to ensure no factual conflicts between them.
- Questions that give conflicting information or mark the same fact as true in one and false in another are not acceptable.

### Language Consistency
- Language must be consistent throughout.
- The same terminology, tone and register must be used across all questions and feedback text.
- Mixing formal and informal language, or switching between different terms for the same concept, is not acceptable.

### Spelling Standard
- Language must use pre-1990 Mozambican Portuguese spelling conventions.
- Pre-1990 Mozambican Portuguese spelling must be used (e.g. "correcto", "acção", "óptimo").
- Brazilian Portuguese, post-1990 European Portuguese, or inconsistent spelling across the quiz are not acceptable.

## 5. HTML5 Interactive Content Characteristics

### Active Learning
- Content must support active learning through interaction (drag-and-drop, quizzes, animations, simulations).
- Drag-and-drop, quizzes, animations and simulations must be included.
- Passive content with no learner interaction is not acceptable.

### Curriculum Progression
- Progression must be aligned with national competencies.
- The national curriculum sequence must be followed in every activity.
- Activities with no link to national competencies are not acceptable.

### Immediate Feedback
- Immediate feedback must be provided for learners.
- Correct/incorrect must be shown immediately after each interaction with a brief explanation.
- Feedback only at the end or not at all is not acceptable.

### Technical Standards
- Content must be fully compliant with Maza HTML5 standards - minimum JS and avoid i-frames.
- Standard HTML5 and CSS must be used. JS must be kept minimal.
- iFrames, heavy JS libraries or code that breaks inside the LP are not acceptable.

### Offline Capability
- Content must be offline-capable - avoid using links to other pages.
- All assets must be bundled inside the module. Test fully offline.
- External links or content that requires an internet connection are not acceptable.

### Navigation
- Simple navigation with clear instructions must be provided.
- Clear Next/Back buttons and instructions must be used on every screen.
- Hidden or unlabelled navigation controls are not acceptable.

### Visual Consistency
- A consistent visual style must be used across modules.
- The same fonts, colours and layout must be used across all modules.
- Different fonts, colours or layouts across modules are not acceptable.

### Accessibility
- Large clickable areas, simple graphics, and clear contrast must be used.
- Large tap targets must be used for all buttons. Graphics must be kept simple with strong text contrast.
- Small buttons or low contrast text are not acceptable.

### Alt Text
- Alternative text must be provided for all images and icons.
- Descriptive alt text must be added to every image and icon.
- Images or icons with no alt text or only a file name are not acceptable.

### Deliverable Format
- The deliverable must be a single self-contained HTML file.
- One HTML file with a descriptive name must be delivered. All audio and images must be embedded as base64. No external files.
- Multiple files, external dependencies, or a file named index.html with no description are not acceptable.

### Instruction Screens
- All instruction screens must include written text and audio narration.
- Every instruction screen must have written text and pre-recorded narration. Include a play button.
- Instruction screens with text only, no audio, or using device-generated speech instead of pre-recorded audio are not acceptable.

### Audio Source
- All audio must be pre-recorded, not generated live by the device.
- Pre-recorded audio files must be used, embedded as base64 in the HTML. A natural-sounding voice must be used, not robotic. Audio quality must be tested before delivery.
- Using Web Speech API or any device-generated voice is not acceptable. Quality varies by device and fails offline.

### Background Music with Narration
- Background music must lower when narration plays.
- Background music must be lowered or muted automatically when narration starts. Remove music that adds no learning value.
- Background music that plays over narration or competes with the speaker is not acceptable.

## 6. Usability & Improvements

### Narration Replay
- The learner must be able to replay the narration audio.
- A replay button must be included so the learner can listen to narration again at any point.
- No replay button, or the learner cannot hear the narration a second time, is not acceptable.

### End Screen Score
- The end screen must show the score with a conditional message.
- The final score must be shown with a motivational message that changes based on performance. It must be read aloud.
- An end screen with only a number and no feedback message, or the same message regardless of score, is not acceptable.

### Results Breakdown
- The end screen must show which questions were correct.
- The learner must be shown which questions they got right and wrong, not just a total score.
- Showing only the total score with no breakdown of individual answers is not acceptable.

### Answer Options
- At least 4 answer options must be provided per question to reduce guessing.
- Fewer than 4 options make it too easy to guess the correct answer and are not acceptable.
